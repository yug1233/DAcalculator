What is Dearness Allowance (DA)?

Dearness Allowance (DA) is an additional payment given to employees to offset the effects of inflation. It is revised twice a year by the government and varies based on location and sector.

How is DA Calculated?

The formula for calculating DA is: DA = (Basic Salary √ó DA Percentage) / 100. The percentage is determined based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI) and economic conditions.

Why is DA Important?

DA helps employees cope with the rising cost of living. It is particularly crucial for government employees, pensioners, and those working in high-inflation areas.

Who Decides the DA Rate?

The government revises the DA rate based on inflation data. It differs for central and state government employees and is typically updated twice a year.
